About

I am a Senior Research Scientist at Google Research in the Future of AI and Society organization. My research is at the intersection of language, AI, and society and I work towards the goal of building safe, and inclusive AI that is usable by the global majority. I currently am focussed on the social grounding of the reponsiveness of generative models in real-world experiences, and contextualizing them with diverse global perspectives and socio-cultural subjectivity.

Prior to this, I was fortunate to receive the NSF Computing Innovation Fellowship to work with Dr. Kai-Wei Chang at UCLA where I also received the DAAD Ainet award for my work. Before this I completed my PhD with Dr. Jeff Phillips at the School of Computing in University of Utah, investigating various facets of high dimensional embeddings such as language models.

I organize multiple workshops at *CL conferences, particularly the Cross Cultural Considerations in NLP workshop which has helped mobilize researchers engaged with multi-cultural NLP of the world, and the HCI + NLP workshop which bridges together interdisciplinary work that is much needed to push the frontiers of language technologies. I am also an advisor and former organizer for the Widening NLP or WiNLP workshop. Reach out to know more or to join us!
